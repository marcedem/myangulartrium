Community Guide

Code of conduct

Artrium is a social community so feel free to post your thoughts, start discussions and be enthusiastic about artworks that inspire you. Your feedback is valuable. Don't hide in the shadows – contribute! Remember, some artists may be at a very early stage in their career, so keep comments constructive. No artholes! Artrium aims for a supportive and positive environment. Artists, please only upload original art. Whether painting, drawing, photography or graffiti, it has to be your own work. Play fair, do not take credit for another person's efforts and look out for one another. All art submitted to Artrium is moderated, but if you spot something amiss, report it and we will investigate. Keep in touch. Follow artrium.me on facebook and instagram for exclusive interviews, featured artworks, news from the art world and artist survival tips. Plus, find out about upcoming events and how to get involved.


How to use the app

Swipe right to discover new artworks. Found one you like? Swipe down to find out who made it, where and when. Read the artist's thoughts about that particular piece and add your comments to the conversation. Found one you love? Hit 'Feedback' in the top right. Share your opinion, see other people's reactions and add it to your personal collection.


Want to join Artrium and have your artwork featured? Easy!

Becoming an Artrium artist is easy. Send an email to artist@artrium.me with a sample of your work. If you’d like to tell us a little more about you, we’d love to see an artist’s CV or short statement, but this is optional. You can also contact us through our website, www.artrium.me As an ‘artist’ member of Artrium you can begin uploading images of your works. If you need help taking the perfect photo (good quality, well-lit), get in touch: we can provide tips or even connect you with a photographer in your neighbourhood.


Artrium is a community platform

All artworks submitted to Artrium are individually checked by our team of moderators. But once the artworks go live, it is over to you. Rate, collect and discuss: artists with a high community rating will be showcased in future events, in-depth features and interviews. With your voice, help curate the art of the future.
